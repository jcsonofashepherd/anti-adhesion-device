"Anti - Adhesion Device" - Team 16
Global Impact
This device would help prevent complications that arise from the pericardial attachment of the heart after open heart surgeries. In America alone, approximately 699,000 open heart surgeries are performed annually (based off of 2005 data from the American Heart Association), and 20% of such cases are reoperations. Moreover, congenital heart defects (CHD) lead the types of birth defects in America, with nearly 40,000 babies or 1% of newborns having these defects.

Individuals with these defects or with heart problems that require cardiac surgery would benefit from this device, preventing pericardial attachment and decreasing reoperations for pericardial adhesions.

Hospitals would stand to benefit from this device also, as it would cut down the number of reoperations needed for the excision of pericardial adhesions, and would save time and resources for the surgeons who would have taken a time scale of hours to excise said adhesions.
